---> Dependency parsing is a technique used in syntactic analysis of sentences to find grammatical relationships between words
---> Instead of breaking the sentence into phrases (like constituency parsing), it directly connects words based on their relationships
--> Each word is connected to another word (called its head) with a labeled arc indicating the type of relationship (dependency)
--> The structure forms a directed tree, usually rooted at the main verb.

Example:
Sentence:

"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

Dependencies:
    "fox" → subject (nsubj) of "jumps"
    "jumps" → root
    "over" → preposition attached to "jumps"
    "dog" → object of the preposition "over"
